Young Life by Edvin Zuckovs

Young Life ‘Enjoy life when you can’ was originally published on the 3rd of June 2021 by a 13 year old young boy from Lincolnshire, Edvin Zuckovs.


Winning global recognition of

Waterstones, Walmart, The Harvard Book Store, Books On The Square (USA), Amazon, Awesome Books, Thrift Books

Junior Non-Fiction



“A book that gets you to think and reflect behind every page.
"The things you do now will always matter in the future, opening more doors with adventures." was one of the eye-opening quotes from the young 13-year-old author (2021).
The book takes the readers on an evocative exploration of the multifaceted stages of youth, prompting a delicate balance between forward-thinking and introspection. This concise yet powerful book serves as a catalyst for the younger generation, encouraging them not only to consider the path ahead but also to reflect on the transformative moments that shape their journey. Through relatable narratives and insightful reflections, "Young Life" inspires young minds to navigate the challenges of the present with an eye toward a purposeful and fulfilling future, but insights the older generations to show reflection.”

Young Life and Education

Young Life by Edvin Zuckovs (aged 13, 2021) was introduced to the school curriculum in the United Kingdom and is used by schools nationally as a ‘case study’. The schools within and outside of the United Kingdom focus on key topics that fit directly into the Personal Social Health and Economics as one of the worlds youngest authors to have published a book.

What Schools in the UK say:
Young Life: “Enjoy Life While You Can.” Provides a compass for the younger generation in our ever increasingly complex society.
Edvin imparts inspirational messages and guidance to the younger generation about life choices, morals, values and there impact towards living a ‘successful’ life. At such as young age, Edvin demonstrates wisdom and ‘life philosophy’ beyond his years! As a ‘mature’ adult I myself thoroughly enjoyed reading his book. Moreover, the class with whom I shared ‘Young Life’ with gained valuable, ‘life lessons’ moving forward. It has been loaned to many pupils and often spoken about during lessons.
Edvin has also visited educational establishments to give talks about the subjects covered within his book. In addition, he has provided mentoring and coaching to pupils in small groups, as well as teaching a range of year groups in a primary school setting about ‘Young Life’.
I’m summary, Edvin provides inspiration as well as a ‘road map’ for the younger generation. If you have young or teenage children ‘Young Life’ will broaden horizons and provoke their life direction in a positive way.
Highly recommend!
